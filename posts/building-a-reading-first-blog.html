Building a Reading-First Blog

Date: April 19, 2026 | Estimated Reading Time: 8 min | Author: You

Good technical blogs are usually simple in the right places. They do not compete with the writing. They make typography, spacing, and information hierarchy carry the interface.

The reference blog you pointed to works because it keeps attention on the text. There is a narrow content column, restrained navigation, generous whitespace, and just enough metadata to help the reader scan.

What to imitate

The useful pattern is not the exact CSS. It is the editorial posture: front page as a stream of essays, article pages optimized for long reading, and almost no chrome competing for attention.

What to avoid

Avoid homepage hero sections that push the actual writing below the fold, noisy card treatments, and heavy client-side logic for a site that mostly serves documents.

That is why this implementation is static, small, and deployable without a build pipeline.
